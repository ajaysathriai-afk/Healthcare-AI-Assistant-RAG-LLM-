Title: Common Cold — Symptoms and Home Care

Overview
Common cold is a viral upper respiratory infection. It is contagious and spreads through droplets and contact.

Symptoms
- Runny or blocked nose
- Sneezing
- Sore throat
- Mild fever (sometimes)
- Mild cough
- Body ache, fatigue

Typical duration
Most colds resolve in 5–10 days.

Home care
- Rest, fluids
- Warm soups/tea
- Steam inhalation
- Saline nasal rinse
- Gargles for sore throat

Prevention
- Handwashing
- Avoid touching face
- Cover cough/sneeze
- Avoid close contact with sick people

Red flags (Seek care)
- Severe fever for >3 days
- Trouble breathing
- Chest pain
- Ear pain
- Symptoms worsening after day 5
- Severe weakness or dehydration
